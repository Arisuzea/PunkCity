Basics & Overview

Vanilla vs PunkCity

What's PunkCity?

PunkCity is a harcore modlist for Cyberpunk 2077. The list changes many of the base game's arcade-style systems with survival mechanics, realistic economic constraints, and tactical combat limitations to closer align gameplay difficulty with the dystopian narrative themes of the setting.

Survival Mode and World Hazard Changes

The mod introduces a survival framework requiring the player character, V, to actively manage physiological requirements. Players must purchase food and drink from interactive street vendors to prevent starvation. Neglecting these mechanics decreases a new core attribute called Nerve, which negatively impacts weapon stability and overall combat performance. Concurrently, environmental dangers are elevated across Night City; enemy gang presence on the streets is significantly more hostile, causing an increase in spontaneous, ambient conflicts.

Netrunning Restrictions and Resource Scarcity

The netrunning system is fundamentally redesigned under the PunkCity framework. Remote, line-of-sight quickhacks are entirely disabled. To hack networks or infrastructure, players must physically interact with terminals or close the distance to targets, shifting netrunning into an active, tactical role. Inventory management is similarly restricted; consumables such as healing items and grenades are converted into finite resources that require manual restocking before embarking on missions.

Weapon and Cyberware Systems

Permanent attachment restrictions found in the retail version of the game are dismantled. Weapon modifications and optics can be detached and swapped between compatible firearms freely without component destruction. Cybernetic enhancements are subject to mechanical wear and operational damage, requiring funds for structural repairs or complete replacements. Furthermore, medical practitioners known as ripperdocs no longer stock universal inventories, instead specializing in specific categories of cybernetics by district.

Vehicle Simulation and Real Estate Economy

Driving mechanics are altered via an Integrated Vehicle System subsystem. This architecture introduces manual control over vehicle functions, allowing players to independently start or kill engines and operate individual doors. The economic loop of the game is expanded by transforming safehouses into recurring financial obligations. Players must pay regular rent settlements to their apartment providers; failure to meet these deadlines results in eviction and subsequent homelessness within the game world.

System Mechanic Base Game (Vanilla) PunkCity Overhaul Suite
Quickhacks Remote, wireless activation Disabled; requires physical proximity
Weapon Mods Permanent upon installation Removable and fully interchangeable
Cyberware Permanent functionality Subject to degradation and breakage
Ripperdocs Universal medical inventories District-specific specialization
Consumables Infinite regeneration and abundance Finite supply; manual restocking required
Housing Economy One-time purchase / perpetual access Recurring rent; eviction mechanics enabled
